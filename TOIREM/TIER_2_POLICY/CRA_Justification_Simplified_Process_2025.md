## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Toirem Canada has implemented a two-tier documentation process that balances CRA compliance requirements with the urgent needs and unique challenges of the Hasidic Jewish community we serve. This document explains why we do not require income verification for assistance under $5,000, while maintaining full compliance with our charitable purposes of poverty relief.

**Legal Authority:** This framework is designed in accordance with:
- **CRA Guidance CG-029** "Relief of poverty and charitable registration" (October 2024 revision)
- **CRA Guidance CG-032** "Registered charities making grants to non-qualified donees" (December 2023)

CG-032 explicitly establishes that grants up to $5,000 are "low risk" and may use simplified documentation including "written communications, such as email records and meeting minutes" rather than formal agreements.

## 1. OUR ASSISTANCE STRUCTURE

### Two-Tier System

| Assistance Level | Documentation Required | Rationale |
|-----------------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Under $5,000** | • Government ID<br>• Completed application<br>• **SIGNED DECLARATION: "I need financial assistance"** | Low-risk per CG-032; applicant's signature replaces income verification |
| **$5,000 and Over** | • All above PLUS<br>• Social Insurance Number<br>• Date of birth<br>• Notice of Assessment (both spouses)<br>• Detailed budget | Medium/high-risk per CG-032; enhanced scrutiny required |

**CRA Authority:** CG-032 (December 2023) explicitly states: "A written agreement may not be necessary for non-recurring grants of $5,000 or less. Other documentation may be enough to satisfy the requirements in very low-risk circumstances."

## 2. JUSTIFICATION FOR SIMPLIFIED PROCESS (Under $5,000)

### CRA's Risk-Based Framework

CRA Guidance CG-029 (October 2024) establishes that documentation requirements should be proportionate to risk. CG-029 states that a charity "may need to apply selection criteria and processes" based on four factors:
1. The **value** of the charitable benefit
2. The **frequency** at which it is provided
3. The **location** where it is provided
4. The charity's **operational circumstances**

For low-value, low-frequency benefits to populations unlikely to include those not in need, CG-029 explicitly states: "The potential of providing non-incidental private benefit is negligible."

### THE CORNERSTONE: Signed Declaration of Need

For amounts under $5,000, we accept the applicant's signed declaration stating "I need financial assistance for my personal day-to-day needs" as sufficient verification of poverty. This declaration:

1. **Is a legal attestation** under penalty of false declaration
2. **Carries weight** in the Hasidic community where one's word is sacred
3. **Reduces barriers** for those unable to navigate complex documentation
4. **Enables rapid response** to emergency situations
5. **Maintains dignity** while ensuring accountability

This approach is similar to statutory declarations used in many government programs and has been accepted by CRA for other charities serving vulnerable populations.

### A. Community-Specific Barriers

#### 1. Language and Literacy Challenges
- **Primary Language**: 70% of our beneficiaries speak Yiddish as their first language
- **Document Complexity**: Tax forms and government documents are in English/French
- **Limited Secular Education**: Many have religious education but limited experience with government paperwork
- **Result**: Requiring complex documentation prevents access to emergency assistance

#### 2. Cultural and Religious Factors
- **Privacy Concerns**: Strong cultural taboos about revealing financial hardship
- **Community Shame**: Seeking help outside the community is stigmatized
- **Trust Issues**: Historical wariness of government documentation
- **Gender Restrictions**: Women may not have independent access to family financial documents

#### 3. Practical Obstacles
- **Single-Income Households**: Mothers managing 6-8 children cannot spend hours gathering documents
- **No Internet Access**: Many Hasidic families don't use internet for religious reasons
- **Banking Limitations**: Some use cash-only systems or informal community lending circles
- **Employment Types**: Many work in religious institutions with non-standard pay documentation

### B. Poverty Indicators Without Income Documents

#### Observable Poverty Markers We Verify:

1. **Family Size Assessment**
   - Number of children (typically 6-8)
   - Ages of children (multiple dependents)
   - Single vs. dual parent household
   - Observable: Large families have inherent higher costs

2. **Address Verification**
   - Living in known low-income housing
   - Multiple families sharing units
   - Basement apartments or subdivided spaces
   - Observable: Housing quality indicates economic status

3. **Community Knowledge**
   - Rabbinical references confirm need
   - Known to other Jewish social services
   - Children receiving subsidized education
   - Observable: Community awareness of family struggles

4. **Request Patterns**
   - Seeking basic necessities only
   - Emergency timing (eviction, utility cutoff)
   - Modest amounts requested
   - Observable: Genuine need presents differently than fraud

## 3. RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES

### How We Ensure Charitable Purposes Without Income Verification:

#### A. Application Controls

1. **Identity Verification**
   - Government-issued photo ID required
   - Address proof mandatory
   - Cross-reference with community directories

2. **Declaration and Acknowledgment**
   - Signed statement of current financial need
   - Stated purpose of requested funds (rent, food, utilities, etc.)
   - Acknowledgment that funds will be used for stated purpose
   - Agreement to follow-up contact if selected

3. **Community Verification**
   - Informal verification through synagogue
   - Check with other Jewish charities
   - Rabbi or community leader awareness

#### B. Assistance Limits

1. **Natural Caps**
   - Maximum $5,000 per year without full documentation
   - Category limits (rent $1,500/month, food $500/month)
   - Frequency restrictions (monthly maximums)

2. **Payment Methods**
   - Checks or e-transfers to recipients (most common)
   - Direct to vendor when practical and preferred
   - Grocery store gift cards for food assistance
   - All methods require stated purpose and recipient acknowledgment
   - No receipts collected — self-declaration model consistent with food banks

#### C. Pattern Analysis

1. **Red Flag Monitoring**
   - Multiple applications from same address
   - Unusual request patterns
   - Amounts exceeding typical need
   - Lack of community connection

2. **Random Audits**
   - 10% of simplified applications selected for verification
   - Post-payment follow-up
   - Community feedback loop
   - Annual pattern review

## 6. COMPARATIVE BEST PRACTICES

### Similar Approaches by Other Charities:

#### Food Banks Model (CRA-Compliant Precedent)
- Daily Bread Food Bank (Toronto): Requires only name, address, date of birth—no income verification
- United Way food banks: Basic intake forms, self-declaration of need
- Self-declaration is standard across the sector
- Focus on immediate relief to populations unlikely to include those not in need
- **CRA Accepted**: These registered charities have operated for decades under this model

#### Self-Declaration Payment Model
- Like food banks, Toirem Canada uses a self-declaration model
- Recipients attest to need AND intended use of funds
- Checks and e-transfers go directly to recipients
- **No receipts required** — recipient's signed acknowledgment is sufficient
- Follow-up calls (not receipt collection) verify outcomes for larger amounts
- **CRA Accepted**: Consistent with poverty relief sector practices
- **Legal Basis**: CG-029 permits simplified verification when "the potential of providing non-incidental private benefit is negligible"

#### Disaster Relief Model
- Red Cross provides emergency aid without income verification
- Salvation Army emergency assistance uses simplified screening
- Focus on crisis intervention
- **CRA Accepted**: Emergency relief recognized as legitimate

#### Religious Community Support
- Many religious charities use community knowledge
- Pastoral verification instead of documents
- Cultural competency in assessment
- **CRA Accepted**: Religious organizations have flexibility

## 9. SPECIFIC TO HASIDIC COMMUNITY NEEDS

### Why This Population Requires Modified Approach:

1. **Documented Higher Costs**: 40-85% higher living expenses (see attached research)
2. **Larger Families**: Average 6-8 children vs. 1.6 Canadian average
3. **Religious Obligations**: $15,000+ annual required expenses
4. **Education Costs**: $7,000-10,000 per child for religious schooling
5. **Limited Government Benefits**: Many benefits cap at 3-4 children

### Traditional Documentation Challenges:

- **Informal Employment**: Religious study stipends, community support
- **Complex Household Economics**: Multiple generation support systems
- **Religious Restrictions**: Sabbath/holiday limitations on document gathering
- **Language Barriers**: Documents in English/French, families operate in Yiddish
