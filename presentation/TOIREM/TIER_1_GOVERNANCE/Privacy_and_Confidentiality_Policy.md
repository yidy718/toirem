# TOIREM CANADA - PRIVACY AND CONFIDENTIALITY POLICY

**Organization:** Toirem Canada
**Date Adopted:** December 30, 2025
**Approved by:** Board of Directors
**Registration Number:** 751018425RR0001

## 1. POLICY STATEMENT

Toirem Canada is committed to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of personal information about our recipients, donors, volunteers, board members, and employees. This policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard personal information in compliance with applicable Canadian privacy legislation, including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and Quebec's Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector.

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all personal information collected, used, or disclosed by Toirem Canada in the course of its charitable activities, including:
- Financial assistance applications and recipient information
- Donor records and contributions
- Volunteer and employee information
- Board member information
- Community partner communications

## 3. DEFINITIONS

### 3.1 Personal Information
Any information about an identifiable individual, including but not limited to:
- Name, address, phone number, email
- Date of birth, social insurance number
- Financial information, income details
- Family composition and circumstances
- Religious affiliation and community connections
- Health information relevant to assistance needs

### 3.2 Sensitive Personal Information
Information requiring extra protection, including:
- Financial hardship details
- Health conditions
- Religious practices
- Children's information

## 4. PRINCIPLES

### 4.1 Accountability
Toirem Canada is responsible for personal information under its control and has designated a Privacy Officer accountable for compliance with this policy.

### 4.2 Identifying Purposes
The purposes for which personal information is collected shall be identified at or before the time the information is collected.

### 4.3 Consent
Knowledge and consent of the individual are required for the collection, use, or disclosure of personal information, except where inappropriate or exempted by law.

### 4.4 Limiting Collection
Collection of personal information shall be limited to that which is necessary for the purposes identified by Toirem Canada.

### 4.5 Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention
Personal information shall not be used or disclosed for purposes other than those for which it was collected, except with consent or as required by law.

### 4.6 Accuracy
Personal information shall be as accurate, complete, and up-to-date as necessary for the purposes for which it is used.

### 4.7 Safeguards
Personal information shall be protected by security safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information.

### 4.8 Openness
Information about our policies and practices relating to the management of personal information shall be made readily available.

### 4.9 Individual Access
Upon request, an individual shall be informed of the existence, use, and disclosure of their personal information and shall be given access to that information.

### 4.10 Challenging Compliance
An individual shall be able to address a challenge concerning compliance with these principles to the designated Privacy Officer.

## 5. COLLECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION

### 5.1 What We Collect
We collect personal information that is necessary to:
- Assess eligibility for financial assistance
- Process and distribute assistance payments
- Maintain records for CRA compliance
- Communicate with recipients about their assistance
- Report to funders and regulatory bodies (in aggregate, anonymized form)
- Improve our programs and services

### 5.2 How We Collect
Personal information is collected through:
- Application forms (paper and electronic)
- Declaration of Need statements
- Phone conversations and in-person meetings
- Community leader referrals
- Follow-up communications

### 5.3 Consent for Collection
By submitting an application for assistance, individuals consent to:
- Collection of necessary personal information
- Verification of information provided
- Use of information for eligibility assessment
- Retention of records as required by law

## 6. USE AND DISCLOSURE OF PERSONAL INFORMATION

### 6.1 Internal Use
Personal information is used internally only by authorized personnel for:
- Eligibility determination
- Assistance calculation and distribution
- Program evaluation and improvement
- Compliance with legal requirements

### 6.2 Disclosure to Third Parties
We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information. Disclosure occurs only:
- With explicit consent
- To comply with legal requirements or court orders
- To protect the rights, property, or safety of individuals
- To authorized service providers bound by confidentiality agreements
- In anonymized, aggregate form for reporting

### 6.3 Special Considerations for Vulnerable Populations
Given the sensitive nature of financial hardship, extra care is taken to:
- Minimize information requests
- Protect dignity of applicants
- Ensure cultural and religious sensitivities are respected
- Maintain strict confidentiality within communities

## 7. SECURITY SAFEGUARDS

### 7.1 Physical Security
- Paper records stored in locked filing cabinets
- Restricted access to offices containing personal information
- Clean desk policy for sensitive documents
- Secure shredding of disposed documents

### 7.2 Electronic Security
- Password-protected computer systems
- Encrypted storage of digital files
- Secure backup procedures
- Regular security updates and patches
- Firewall and antivirus protection

### 7.3 Administrative Security
- Confidentiality agreements for all staff and volunteers
- Limited access on need-to-know basis
- Regular privacy training for personnel
- Clear procedures for handling information requests
- Incident response procedures for breaches

## 8. RETENTION AND DISPOSAL

### 8.1 Retention Periods
- Recipient files: 7 years from last assistance provided (CRA requirement)
- Donor records: 7 years from donation date
- Unsuccessful applications: 2 years
- General inquiries: 1 year

### 8.2 Secure Disposal
- Paper records: Cross-cut shredding
- Electronic records: Secure deletion/overwriting
- Hardware: Professional data destruction services

## 9. ACCESS AND CORRECTION

### 9.1 Access Requests
Individuals may request access to their personal information by:
- Submitting a written request to the Privacy Officer
- Providing sufficient information to locate records
- Paying any applicable fees (waived for recipients)

### 9.2 Response Timeline
- Acknowledgment within 3 business days
- Full response within 30 days
- Extensions only with notification and justification

### 9.3 Corrections
Individuals may request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete information. Disputed information will be noted in the file.

## 10. SPECIAL PROVISIONS FOR CHARITABLE ASSISTANCE

### 10.1 Dignity and Respect
All personal information collection respects the dignity of individuals seeking assistance:
- Minimal information requirements
- Sensitive handling of financial hardship details
- No unnecessary intrusion into personal circumstances

### 10.2 Community Confidentiality
Recognizing the close-knit nature of the communities we serve:
- Strict confidentiality between applicants
- No discussion of individual cases outside authorized personnel
- Protection from stigma or embarrassment

### 10.3 Declaration-Based System
Our simplified declaration system minimizes privacy intrusions while maintaining program integrity:
- Self-declaration of need reduces documentation requirements
- Trust-based approach respects individual privacy
- Verification only when necessary

## 11. COOKIES AND WEBSITE PRIVACY

### 11.1 Website Information
Our website may collect:
- IP addresses for security purposes
- Usage statistics in aggregate form
- Contact information voluntarily provided

### 11.2 Cookies
We use cookies only for:
- Session management
- Security purposes
- Improving user experience
Users can disable cookies in browser settings.

## 13. TRAINING AND AWARENESS

### 13.1 Staff Training
All staff and volunteers receive:
- Initial privacy training upon joining
- Annual refresher training
- Updates on policy changes
- Specific training for sensitive roles

### 13.2 Board Oversight
The Board of Directors:
- Reviews this policy annually
- Receives reports on privacy matters
- Ensures adequate resources for privacy protection

## 14. BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 14.1 Breach Response
In case of a privacy breach:
1. Immediate containment and assessment
2. Notification to affected individuals if risk of harm
3. Notification to Privacy Commissioner if required
4. Documentation and lessons learned
5. Implementation of preventive measures

### 14.2 Breach Records
All breaches are documented including:
- Nature of breach
- Information affected
- Individuals impacted
- Response actions taken
- Preventive measures implemented
