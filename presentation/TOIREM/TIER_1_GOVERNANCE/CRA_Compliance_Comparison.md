## The CRA Requirement

To operate as a registered charity providing "relief of poverty," the CRA requires:

> "A charity relieving poverty must direct its resources to people who lack the necessities of life... The charity should be able to demonstrate how it determines that a person is in need."

## Approach A: Ad-Hoc Thresholds (HIGH RISK)

### How It Works
- Thresholds set based on "what feels right" or "community consensus"
- No documentation of how numbers were derived
- May use round numbers ($100,000, $150,000) without justification
- Relies on Inspector discretion without clear guidelines

### Example
> "A family of 6 earning $120,000 qualifies because everyone knows Orthodox families need more."

### CRA Problems with This Approach

| Issue | CRA Concern | Risk |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **No methodology** | "How did you arrive at these numbers?" | Audit finding |
| **No sources** | "What evidence supports these thresholds?" | Potential revocation |
| **Subjective criteria** | "Different inspectors could reach different conclusions" | Inconsistent application |
| **High thresholds** | "Why is $120,000 considered poverty?" | Private benefit concerns |
| **No poverty line reference** | "How does this relate to MBM?" | Failure to demonstrate need |

### Worst Case Scenario
CRA could determine that assistance is going to people who are NOT in poverty, which means:
- Organization is providing **private benefit**, not charitable relief
- Charitable status could be **revoked**
- Directors could face **personal liability**
- Donors could lose **tax receipts retroactively**

---

## Approach B: Research-Backed Thresholds (LOW RISK)

### How It Works
- Start with **official government baseline** (Statistics Canada SHS: $65,344)
- Document **each additional cost** with sources
- Show **clear calculation methodology**
- Reference **official poverty measures** (MBM, IRIS) for context
- Maintain **audit trail** of all data sources

### Example
> "A family of 6 with verified income of $120,000 qualifies because:
> - Base cost of living (StatsCan SHS 2023): $88,214
> - + Kosher food premium (+40%): $8,400
> - + Jewish education (4 children Ã— $8,500): $34,000
> - + Religious obligations: $15,966
> - + Housing premium (4BR near synagogue): $12,000
> - **Total documented need: $158,580**
> - Income shortfall: $38,580
> - Assistance: 60% of shortfall = $23,148"

### Why CRA Accepts This Approach

| Element | CRA Benefit | Evidence |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Government baseline** | Anchored to official data | StatsCan Table 11-10-0222-01 |
| **Documented premiums** | Each cost justified separately | CFIA, CMHC, Federation CJA |
| **Poverty line context** | Shows relationship to MBM | $49,244 MBM vs. $158,580 actual need |
| **Clear formula** | Reproducible calculations | 60% of verified shortfall |
| **Verification requirements** | NOA required for $5,000+ | Prevents abuse |

### CRA Precedent
The CRA has accepted that certain communities face higher costs:
- Disability-related expenses above standard
- Remote/Northern community cost premiums
- Cultural/religious requirements creating unavoidable costs

**Key:** You must DOCUMENT these costs with credible sources.

## Responding to Partner Objections

### "This is too complicated"

**Response:** CRA compliance IS complicated. The alternative is losing charitable status. This research protects everyone.

### "We've always done it the other way"

**Response:** Past practice doesn't equal CRA compliance. Many charities have lost status for exactly this reason. We should fix it now, not after an audit.

### "Our thresholds are similar to the research anyway"

**Response:** Great! Then documenting them properly costs us nothing and protects us completely. The numbers aren't the issue â€” the documentation is.

### "The Inspector knows the community"

**Response:** Inspector knowledge is valuable, but CRA requires DOCUMENTED criteria. An Inspector's judgment should be informed by research, not replace it.

### "This will slow down approvals"

**Response:** The verification requirements only apply to amounts over $5,000. Under $5,000 still only needs a signed declaration. Most families won't be affected.

### "What if our current thresholds are wrong?"

**Response:** Then we adjust them based on evidence. It's better to have defensible thresholds than indefensible ones â€” even if they're different from what we used before.
